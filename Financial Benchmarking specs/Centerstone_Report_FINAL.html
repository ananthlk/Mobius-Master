Section 1: About This Report

Organization Profile

Your organization is a comprehensive community behavioral health system classified as a Community Mental Health Center (CMHC) operating in Bradenton, FL. The visible Medicaid claims portfolio is dominated by two service lines: assertive community treatment at 47.8% of revenue ($1,189,120) and outpatient therapy at 41.5% ($1,034,634), with assessment (9.3%), case management (0.7%), and medication management (0.2%) comprising the remainder. The organization does not hold a residential taxonomy and does not bill crisis stabilization codes in this dataset.

Data Scope

This report analyzes $2,490,235.35 in Florida Medicaid fee-for-service claims billed through professional servicing clinicians during 2024. This represents one component of your organization's total revenue.

What is visible: Within this $2.5 million, we benchmark codes where sufficient peer comparison data exists.

What is not visible: MCO contract payments, facility-level billing, commercial insurance, grants, and other funding sources are not included.

The Four Metrics

Metric What It Measures
Rate per Claim What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal.
Patients per Clinician How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves. This is your caseload signal.
Visits per Patient How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment engagement signal.
Revenue per Patient Total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal.

Sector Gap Context

An important context for this report: the CMHC sector in Florida systematically collects less per claim than the broader Medicaid market on several key codes. For example, CMHC median payment for targeted case management (T1017) is $49.58 -- 20% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. For medication management (T1015), CMHCs collect $65.02 vs the market's $70.18 (7% below). This means that performing "at the CMHC median" may still represent underperformance relative to the broader market. Throughout this report, we compare against both your sector peers (similar CMHCs) and the full Florida Medicaid market. The aspirational benchmark is closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median.

Rate Layer Framing

When published fee schedule rates are available, this report frames rate performance across three layers: (1) the FL Medicaid published rate -- the maximum AHCA will pay, (2) the All FL Medicaid average paid rate -- what the industry actually collects after MCO discounts and billing variation, and (3) your organization's average paid rate. The gap between published and industry rates reflects structural compression the entire market faces. The gap between industry and your rate is the org-specific variance that is most actionable.

Peer Groups

Your organization was matched to 10 look-alike peers using a service-line revenue mix similarity algorithm, with similarity scores ranging from 0.747 to 0.990. In addition to this primary peer set, the report compares against five secondary groups: All FL Medicaid Organizations, Organization-billed providers, Similar CMHC Peers, Similar Size Organizations, and Similar Market peers. The look-alike group provides the tightest operational comparison; the All FL Medicaid group establishes the industry-wide benchmark.

How to Read the Charts

In each chart, the diamond marks your organization's value. The blue line marks the peer median. The purple line marks the look-alike peer median. The gray band spans P25 to P75.

Section 3: Code-Level Deep Dives

Behavioral Therapy Services (H2019)

Service Line: Outpatient Therapy

Rate per Claim -- At Market: Your organization collects $77.07 per claim, 6.0% above the look-alike peer median of $72.73 (n=5) and 7.9% above the All FL Medicaid median of $71.43 (n=614). The published rate for this code is $21.87 per 15-minute unit; the All FL industry median of $71.43 already reflects substantial compression from the published schedule, meaning your $77.07 rate sits above the industry's already-compressed level. Your org-to-industry gap is favorable at +7.9%.

Patients per Clinician -- On Track: Your clinicians carry 419.8 patients each, which is 99.0% above the All FL median of 211.0 -- a position that warrants investigation into whether this reflects an efficient model or potential capacity strain.

Utilization -- At Market: Visits per patient of 2.13 sit near the CMHC peer median of 2.04 (n=86) but below the All FL median of 2.51 (n=614).

Revenue per Patient -- On Track: Revenue per patient of $163.92 is above the look-alike peer median of $116.81 and the CMHC median of $137.38, though below the All FL median of $180.81.

Trend: Rate moved from $70.99 (2019) to $77.07 (2024). Signal: steady.

Rate Advantage vs Look-Alikes: $87,391 annual rate advantage vs look-alike peers.

Key Question: Given that the rate premium is specific to this code and not replicated across other outpatient codes, your team should investigate whether the higher per-claim amount reflects longer session durations, a particular modifier pattern, or favorable contract terms that could be leveraged on other codes.

Caveat: The $77.07 per-claim figure may reflect multiple 15-minute units billed per claim (published rate is $21.87 per 15-minute unit), so the rate premium could partly represent longer sessions rather than higher per-unit reimbursement.

Section 4: Cross-Cutting Observations

4a. Sector Gap

The CMHC sector in Florida systematically collects less than the broader Medicaid market on several codes visible in this report. For targeted case management (T1017), the CMHC median of $49.58 sits 20.3% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. Your organization's rate of $49.48 is at the CMHC median -- meaning it matches peers who are themselves underperforming relative to the broader market. The aspirational goal is not simply matching CMHC peers but closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median, and ultimately positioning above it.

4b. Rate Layers

The rate premium on behavioral therapy (H2019) illustrates the three-layer framing clearly: the published rate is $21.87 per 15-minute unit, the All FL industry median is $71.43 per claim (reflecting multi-unit billing), and your organization collects $77.07. This means your favorable position is relative to an industry level that is already far above the per-unit published rate. By contrast, on targeted case management (T1017), the published rate is $14.82 per 15-minute unit, the industry median is $62.20, and your rate is $49.48 -- placing you below the industry's compressed level. The rate premium is code-specific, not organization-wide.

Caseload Pattern

A consistent pattern of low patients-per-clinician appears across several codes: targeted case management at 94.0 vs the CMHC peer median of 275.0 (65.8% below), and assertive community treatment at 786.0 vs the All FL median of 546.0 (44.0% above but with only 2 billing clinicians). For T1017, the caseload gap is the dominant signal -- far more pronounced than the rate gap -- and raises the question of whether staffing levels have outpaced patient volume growth, whether patients are being routed to alternative service lines, or whether a high-touch care model intentionally limits panel sizes.

Rate Outlier Pattern

The behavioral therapy (H2019) rate of $77.07 is 7.9% above the All FL median, while targeted case management (T1017) at $49.48 sits 20.5% below the All FL median -- demonstrating that the rate position is code-specific and not an organization-wide pattern. Your team should investigate whether the H2019 premium can be attributed to specific contract terms, modifier patterns, or session durations that might inform rate improvement strategies on underperforming codes.

Revenue Concentration

The top three service lines -- assertive community treatment (47.8%), outpatient therapy (41.5%), and assessment (9.3%) -- account for 98.6% of visible revenue. Critically, ACT alone at 47.8% has only 22 organizations billing statewide, meaning the largest revenue stream operates with limited benchmarking depth. Diversification of the benchmarked portfolio will improve as more peer data becomes available for ACT and if crisis stabilization enters the claims mix.

Section 5: Caveats & Limitations

Methodology

Peer groups are constructed using parallel single-dimension cuts (All FL Medicaid, organization type, size, market, taxonomy) with a minimum organization threshold per group. Look-alike peers are matched using cosine similarity on service-line revenue mix vectors, with a minimum revenue threshold of $10,000 and a similarity score of 0.75+ indicating a comparable organization. A detailed methodology compendium documenting the data sources, algorithms, peer matching logic, and statistical methods used in this report is available on request.

Per-Claim vs Per-Unit

All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes, a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units.

Modifier Rollup

Assessment codes (H0031, H0032) combine all modifier variations into a single per-code figure.

Residential Gap

Residential per-diem codes are absent from this dataset; your organization does not hold a residential taxonomy, and these codes are not included in the analysis.

Volume Limitations

Codes 99221 (39 claims) and H0046 (65 claims) have fewer than 100 claims in the analysis period; badge assignments and peer comparisons for these codes should be interpreted with additional caution.

This report observes and questions. It does not diagnose, recommend, or speculate. When a metric is outside the typical range (below P25 or above P75), the report states the observation and asks what might be driving it.
