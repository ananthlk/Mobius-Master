Four Key Metrics

Metric Definition
Rate per Claim What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal.
Patients per Clinician How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves. This is your caseload signal.
Visits per Patient How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment engagement signal.
Revenue per Patient Total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal.

Peer Groups

Your organization is compared against 10 look-alike peers selected by similarity of Medicaid revenue mix, with similarity scores ranging from 0.7639 to 0.8559. In addition to look-alike peers, five secondary peer groups provide broader context: All FL Medicaid Orgs, Similar CMHCs, Similar Size Orgs, Similar Market, and Same Classification. Each secondary group captures a different dimension of comparability and may include organizations with different billing structures or service models.

How to Read the Charts

In each chart, the diamond marks your organization's value. The blue line marks the peer median. The purple line marks the look-alike peer median. The gray band spans P25 to P75.

Badge Legend

Badge Meaning

Key Limitation

Rate per claim measures what your organization is paid per claim submission, not per 15-minute unit of service. Organizations that bill longer sessions will show higher per-claim amounts.

How We Frame Outliers

When a metric falls below P25 or above P75 of the peer distribution, this report does not interpret the cause or speculate on the reason. It raises the observation as a question for your team to investigate using internal operational and billing data. Outlier positions may reflect favorable contract terms, billing structure differences, service model choices, data limitations, or other factors that cannot be determined from claims data alone.

This means the rate premium is code-specific to H2019, not an organization-wide pattern, and your team should investigate what is unique about H2019 billing -- session duration, unit bundling, or contract terms -- that does not apply to other codes.

Revenue Concentration

The top three service lines -- outpatient therapy (40.8%), ACT (26.1%), and crisis stabilization (23.1%) -- account for 90.0% of visible revenue. Of that, 49.2% (ACT plus crisis) has limited or no peer benchmarking data, which means nearly half of your organization's visible Medicaid FFS revenue cannot be evaluated against peers in this report.

Section 5: Caveats and Methodology Notes

Peer Methodology

The look-alike methodology matches organizations by the similarity of their visible Medicaid revenue mix. It identifies peers who bill a comparable blend of outpatient therapy, ACT, and case management codes. However, it does not directly match on organizational structure. Your organization operates a crisis stabilization unit (23.1% of visible revenue) and is registered as a residential treatment facility, but most look-alike peers lack these service lines. For crisis and residential services, a service-specific peer group would provide more meaningful comparison.

Rate per Claim vs Rate per Unit

All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes, a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units.

Modifier Rollup

Assessment codes (H0031, H0032) combine all modifier variations into a single per-code figure.

Residential Gap

Your organization is registered as a residential treatment facility, but no residential per-diem codes appear in this data, so residential revenue is entirely absent from this benchmarking.

Volume Limitations

Three codes have fewer than 100 claims -- H0031 (91 claims), H0032 (68 claims), and 99214 (45 claims) -- and metrics for these codes carry greater uncertainty due to small sample sizes.
