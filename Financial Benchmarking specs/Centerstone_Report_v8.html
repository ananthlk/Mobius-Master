PROBLEM 3 Clinician Churn and Burnout

The sector picture: CMHCs carry the sector's most complex patients at the lowest effective rates — a combination that compresses clinician productivity and contributes to burnout. When rates are lower, clinicians must see more patients to generate the same revenue. When patients are higher-acuity, each visit takes more clinical time. The result: burnout that drives churn, which further constrains capacity.

Where you sit: Critical load across every major service line

This is THE story for Centerstone. Panel sizes (beneficiaries per clinician) are at 2–4x the CMHC sector norm on every major code. The volume engine that drives your revenue is running on a very small number of clinicians.

A rate of $5.61 vs CMHC P50 of $30.38 and All FL P50 of $33.32 is anomalous (-83.2% below market). With 692 claims and 498 beneficiaries, this code has real volume but near-zero effective payment. Review modifier usage, place-of-service codes, and claim adjudication detail.

Impact: $1.19M in revenue concentration. Does the org have contingency plans for ACT disruption?

Methodology

Data source: FL Medicaid FFS claims (DOGE/Medicaid Provider Spending), 2024. MCO encounter data not included.

Org identification: Claims matched by billing NPI to organizational entity via NPPES. Professional servicing NPIs only (entity_type_code = 1).

Peer groups: CMHC sector (86 orgs), All FL Medicaid (full market), Similar Size, Similar Market, Same Classification, Look-alike peers (10 orgs, cosine similarity 0.75–0.99 on service-line revenue mix).

Metrics: Payment per claim (rate), revenue per beneficiary (engagement depth), claims per beneficiary (visit frequency), beneficiaries per clinician (panel size / productivity).

Sector context: Sector-level numbers ($8.1M rate gap, 23.6%→5.0% leakage, $157M upper-bound leakage gap) are from the FL CMHC Landscape Report v4, which benchmarks the full CMHC sector against all non-CMHC FL Medicaid providers.

Limitations: This report shows FL Medicaid FFS only — a partial view. Organization-level conclusions should be validated against internal data. MCO encounter data and facility-level billing are not captured. Beneficiary counts are not deduplicated across service codes. Centerstone uses H2000 for psychiatric evaluation rather than H0031; the assessment service line includes both H0032 and H2000.
