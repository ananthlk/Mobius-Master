### Data Scope

This report analyzes $3,277,908.71 in Florida Medicaid fee-for-service claims billed through professional servicing clinicians during 2024. This represents one component of your organization's total revenue.

**What is visible:** Within this $3.3 million, we benchmark codes where sufficient peer comparison data exists.

**What is not visible:** MCO contract payments, facility-level billing, commercial insurance, grants, and other funding sources are not included.

### The Four Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Rate per Claim** | What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal. |
| **Patients per Clinician** | How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves. This is your caseload signal. |
| **Visits per Patient** | How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment engagement signal. |
| **Revenue per Patient** | Total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal. |

### Peer Groups

Your organization is compared against 10 look-alike peers selected by a multi-dimensional similarity algorithm, with similarity scores ranging from 0.8326 to 0.9464. In addition to look-alike peers, this report includes five secondary peer groups: All FL Medicaid Organizations, Organization-billed providers, Similar Behavioral Health Specialty peers, Similar Size Organizations (Medium), and Similar Market (Moderate) density providers.

### How to Read the Charts

In each chart, the diamond marks your organization's value. The blue line marks the peer median. The purple line marks the look-alike peer median. The gray band spans P25 to P75.

### Badge Legend

| Badge | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Strong** | Above market median, holding steady or pulling ahead |
| **At Market** | Near the median, stable position |
| **Watch** | Above median but converging toward it, or below median but recovering |
| **Concern** | Below the median on most peer comparisons |

### Sector Context

An important context for this report: the behavioral health specialty sector in Florida systematically collects less per claim than the broader Medicaid market on several key codes. For targeted case management, the BH specialty sector median is $59.78 -- below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. For mental health assessment, BH specialty peers collect $36.86 vs the market's $31.59. This means that performing "at the sector median" may still represent underperformance relative to the broader market on certain codes. Throughout this report, we compare against both your sector peers (similar behavioral health specialty organizations) and the full Florida Medicaid market. The aspirational benchmark is closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median.

### Rate Layer Context

When published fee schedule rates are available, this report frames rate performance across three layers: (1) the FL Medicaid published rate -- the maximum AHCA will pay, (2) the All FL Medicaid average paid rate -- what the industry actually collects after MCO discounts and billing variation, and (3) your organization's average paid rate. The gap between published and industry rates reflects structural compression the entire market faces. The gap between industry and your rate is the org-specific variance that is most actionable.

### Key Limitation

Rate per claim measures what your organization is paid per claim submission, not per 15-minute unit of service. Organizations that bill longer sessions will show higher per-claim amounts.

## Section 4: Cross-Cutting Observations

### 4a. Sector Gap

Your organization operates as a behavioral health specialty provider, a sector that in Florida systematically collects less per claim than the broader Medicaid market on several key codes. For targeted case management, the BH specialty peer median of $59.78 is below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. Your organization's rate of $44.86 falls below even the sector median, creating a compounded gap: your rate is below your sector, which itself is below the full market. The aspirational benchmark throughout this report is closing the gap first to the BH specialty sector median, and then to the All FL Medicaid median.

### 4b. Rate Layers

Where published fee schedule rates are available, a three-layer pattern emerges. For mental health assessment, the published rate is $126.11 per event, the All FL Medicaid industry median is $31.59, and your organization collects $21.40 -- showing both severe industry-wide compression (the market collects a fraction of the published rate) and an additional org-specific gap on top of that structural compression. For targeted case management, the published rate is $14.82 per 15-minute unit while the industry median is $62.20 per claim (reflecting multi-unit billing), and your rate of $44.86 represents the org-to-industry variance that is most actionable. The published rate establishes context but the org-to-industry gap is where investigation should focus.

### 4c. Rate Concentration

Behavioral therapy services is the only code where your organization collects above the All FL Medicaid median ($79.53 vs $71.43). On every other benchmarked code -- targeted case management, psychosocial rehabilitation, mental health assessment, and office visits -- your organization is below the market median, indicating that the rate advantage is code-specific rather than organization-wide.

### 4d. Revenue Concentration

The top three service lines -- case management (29.3%), psychosocial rehabilitation (26.5%), and assertive community treatment (24.3%) -- account for 80.1% of visible revenue. ACT alone represents 24.3% with limited peer benchmarking. This concentration means that rate and utilization movements on just one or two codes can materially shift overall financial performance.

## Section 5: Caveats and Methodology

**Peer Methodology:** Look-alike peers are selected using a multi-dimensional similarity algorithm that considers organization type, size, market density, service mix, and taxonomy. The 10 look-alike peers for your organization have similarity scores from 0.8326 to 0.9464. Secondary peer groups use single-dimension matching (all FL Medicaid, organization-billed, BH specialty, size, and market density).

**Per-Claim vs Per-Unit:** All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes, a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units.

**Modifier Rollup:** Assessment codes (H0031) combine all modifier variations into a single per-code figure.

**Residential Gap:** Residential per-diem codes are absent from this analysis; your organization does not have a residential taxonomy in the data, so this service category is not represented.

**Volume Limitations:** Office Visit Level 2 (99212) has only 16 claims, making its benchmarks directional at best.
