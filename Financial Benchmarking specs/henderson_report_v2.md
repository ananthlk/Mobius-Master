## Section 1: About This Report

### Organization Profile

Your organization is a community behavioral health provider in Broward County, Florida, classified as a BH Specialty organization. Visible Medicaid FFS revenue is concentrated in case management (29.3% of total paid), psychosocial rehabilitation (26.5%), and assertive community treatment (24.3%), with outpatient therapy contributing 18.8%. The organization does not bill residential per-diem codes and does not bill crisis stabilization services in this data.

### Sector Context

An important context for this report: behavioral health specialty organizations in Florida often collect less per claim than the broader Medicaid market on several key codes. For example, Similar BH_SPECIALTY Peers have a median payment for targeted case management (T1017) of $59.78 (n=40) compared to the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20 (n=288). For psychosocial rehabilitation (H2017), BH_SPECIALTY peers collect $103.87 (n=35) vs the All FL Medicaid median of $120.93 (n=285). This means that performing "at the BH_SPECIALTY peer median" may still represent underperformance relative to the broader market. Throughout this report, we compare first against All FL Medicaid and then provide secondary context from Similar BH_SPECIALTY Peers. The aspirational benchmark is closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median.

### Rate Layer Framing

When published fee schedule rates are available, this report frames rate performance across three layers: (1) the FL Medicaid published rate -- the maximum AHCA will pay, (2) the All FL Medicaid average paid rate -- what the industry actually collects after MCO discounts and billing variation, and (3) your organization's average paid rate. The gap between published and industry rates reflects structural compression the entire market faces. The gap between industry and your rate is the org-specific variance that is most actionable.

### Data Scope

This report analyzes $3,277,908.71 in Florida Medicaid fee-for-service claims billed through professional servicing clinicians during 2024. This represents one component of your organization's total revenue.

**What is visible:** Within this $3.3 million, we benchmark codes where sufficient peer comparison data exists.

**What is not visible:** MCO contract payments, facility-level billing, commercial insurance, grants, and other funding sources are not included.

### The Four Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Rate per Claim** | What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal. |
| **Patients per Clinician** | How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves. This is your caseload signal. |
| **Visits per Patient** | How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment engagement signal. |
| **Revenue per Patient** | Total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal. |

### Peer Groups

Your organization is compared against 10 look-alike peers (similarity range: 0.83 to 0.95), identified based on service mix, size, and market characteristics. Secondary comparisons include All FL Medicaid Orgs, Organization-billed providers, Similar BH_SPECIALTY Peers, Similar Size Orgs (Medium), and Similar Market (Moderate). All FL Medicaid is the primary benchmark throughout this report; look-alike and org-type peers provide secondary context.

### How to Read the Charts

In each chart, the diamond marks your organization's value. The blue line marks the peer median. The purple line marks the look-alike peer median. The gray band spans P25 to P75.

### Badge Legend

| Badge | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Strong** | Above market median, holding steady or pulling ahead |
| **At Market** | Near the median, stable position |
| **Watch** | Above median but converging toward it, or below median but recovering |
| **Concern** | Below the median on most peer comparisons |

### Key Limitation

Rate per claim measures what your organization is paid per claim submission, not per 15-minute unit of service. Organizations that bill longer sessions will show higher per-claim amounts.

## Section 4: Cross-Cutting Observations

### Caseload Patterns

Your organization shows a distinctive split in caseload intensity: targeted case management carries 595.6 patients per clinician (well above the All FL median of 258.0), while mental health assessment shows 200.6 (well above the median of 83.0). These high caseloads coincide with below-market rates on both codes. Could the high volume per clinician be associated with shorter encounter durations, which in turn produce lower per-claim payments on time-based codes?

### Rate Performance Is Code-Specific, Not Organization-Wide

The rate picture is not uniform across services. Behavioral therapy (H2019) runs above the All FL Medicaid median at $79.53 vs $71.43, while targeted case management (T1017) runs well below at $44.86 vs $62.20 and mental health assessment (H0031) at $21.40 vs $31.59. The rate premium on H2019 does not extend to other codes, suggesting that the factors driving above-market performance on therapy are specific to that service's billing structure or session patterns.

### Revenue Concentration and Benchmarking Gaps

The top three service lines -- case management (29.3%), psychosocial rehabilitation (26.5%), and ACT (24.3%) -- account for 80.1% of visible revenue. Of these, ACT (24.3%) has limited peer benchmarking data, meaning roughly a quarter of visible revenue falls outside the scope of meaningful rate comparison. This concentration creates a material blind spot in assessing overall rate performance.

## Section 5: Methodology and Caveats

### Peer Methodology

All peer comparisons are based on Florida Medicaid fee-for-service claims data for the 2024 analysis period. Peer groups are constructed from organizations billing the same codes through professional servicing clinicians. Look-alike peers are selected based on multi-dimensional similarity scoring across service mix, organizational size, and market characteristics.

### Per-Claim vs Per-Unit

All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes, a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units.

### Modifier Rollup

Assessment codes (H0031) combine all modifier variations into a single per-code figure.

### Residential Gap

Your organization does not bill residential per-diem codes in this data; residential services are absent from this benchmarking analysis.

### Volume Limitations

Office Visit L2 (99212) has only 16 claims and only 1 look-alike peer, making rate comparisons for this code directional at best rather than statistically robust.
