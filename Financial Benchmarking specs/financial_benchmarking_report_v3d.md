### The Four Metrics

- **Rate per Claim ($/claim):** What you are paid on average each time a claim is submitted for a given service. A higher rate means more revenue per encounter.
- **Patients per Clinician (caseload):** How many unique patients each clinician serves. A larger caseload can mean greater efficiency or capacity strain.
- **Visits per Patient (treatment frequency):** How many claims are filed per patient. Higher frequency may reflect clinical intensity or longer treatment episodes.
- **Revenue per Patient ($/patient):** Total revenue generated per patient served. This combines the effects of rate, visit frequency, and billing structure into a single measure.

### How to Read the Dashboard

Each code receives four badges, one per metric:

| Badge | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Strong** | Above market median on this metric; position is stable or improving |
| **At Market** | Near the market median; no immediate concern |
| **Concern** | Below market median on this metric; warrants investigation |

Trend arrows indicate direction over 2019--2024:

| Arrow | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| &#8593; | Pulling ahead / Recovering | Your rate is rising faster than peers |
| &#8594; | Steady / Converging | Your rate is tracking with peers or closing a gap |
| &#8595; | Falling behind | Your rate is declining relative to peers |

**Volume caution** flag appears when a code has fewer than 100 claims, meaning the sample is too small for confident benchmarking.

### Key Limitation

Rate per claim measures what you are paid per claim submission, not per 15-minute session. Organizations that bill longer sessions will show higher per-claim amounts. A rate that appears "well above" the median may partly reflect session length rather than a richer fee schedule.

## 5. Caveats and Methodology

### Peer Methodology

The look-alike methodology matches organizations by the similarity of their visible Medicaid revenue mix. It identifies peers who bill a comparable blend of outpatient therapy, ACT, and case management codes. However, it does not directly match on organizational structure. Your organization operates a crisis stabilization unit (23.1% of visible revenue) and is registered as a residential treatment facility, but most look-alike peers lack these service lines. For crisis and residential services, a service-specific peer group would provide more meaningful comparison.

### Per-Claim vs Per-Unit

Rate per claim measures what you are paid per claim submission, not per 15-minute unit of service. The underlying data source (FL Medicaid professional claims) does not include a units-of-service field. Organizations that bill longer sessions or bundle multiple units into a single claim line will show higher per-claim amounts. This is particularly relevant for H2019, where the $143.65 per-claim rate may partly reflect session length rather than a richer fee schedule.

### Modifier Rollup for H0031 and H0032

Assessment codes H0031 and H0032 can be billed with various modifiers (e.g., indicating service setting or clinician type). This report combines all billing variations of each code into a single rate. If your organization bills these codes predominantly with a modifier that carries a different rate, the blended per-claim amount may not be directly comparable to peers who use a different modifier mix.

### Residential Revenue Not Captured

Your organization is registered as a residential treatment facility (taxonomy 251S00000X), but no residential per-diem codes appear in the claims data used for this report. Residential revenue is not reflected in the totals or service line breakdown above.

### Volume Limitations

Three codes (H0032 with 68 claims, H0031 with 91 claims, and 99214 with 45 claims) fall below the 100-claim threshold for confident benchmarking. Their badge assessments should be interpreted with caution, as small changes in billing volume can meaningfully shift per-claim rates.
