Your organization's total annual operations are significantly larger than the $1.2 million visible in this data. The benchmarks and findings below apply only to this specific billing channel.

**What is visible:** Within this $1.2 million slice, we benchmark six procedure codes where sufficient peer comparison data exists. The majority of revenue from these programs -- residential per-diem payments, MCO contract payments, facility-level crisis billing, and non-Medicaid funding -- does not appear in this data. This report should be read as a benchmarking of your professional outpatient Medicaid billing, not as a comprehensive financial assessment.

**Claim count note:** Claim counts in this report reflect claims billed through professional servicing clinicians with a Florida practice location. Your organization's total claim volume for some codes is higher -- for example, 2,904 total H2019 claims in 2024, of which 1,254 flow through professional clinicians and are used for benchmarking.

### The Four Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Rate per Claim** | What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal. |
| **Patients per Clinician** | How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves. This is your caseload signal. |
| **Visits per Patient** | How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment engagement signal. |
| **Revenue per Patient** | Total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal. |

### Peer Groups

Your organization is compared against 10 look-alike peers selected by the similarity of their Medicaid revenue mix, with similarity scores ranging from 0.76 to 0.86 (1.0 = identical). Look-alike peers are organizations that bill a comparable blend of outpatient therapy, ACT, and case management codes. In addition, five secondary peer groups provide broader context: All FL Medicaid Organizations, Similar CMHCs, Similar Size Organizations, Similar Market Organizations, and Same Classification Organizations.

### Sector Gap Context

An important context for this report: the CMHC sector in Florida systematically collects less per claim than the broader Medicaid market on several key codes. For example, the CMHC median payment for targeted case management is $49.58 -- 20% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. For medication management, CMHCs collect $65.02 vs the market's $70.18 (7% below). This means that performing "at the CMHC median" may still represent underperformance relative to the broader market. Throughout this report, we compare against both your sector peers and the full Florida Medicaid market. The aspirational benchmark is closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median.

### Rate Layers

When published fee schedule rates are available, this report frames rate performance across three layers: (1) the FL Medicaid published rate -- the maximum AHCA will pay, (2) the All FL Medicaid average paid rate -- what the industry actually collects after MCO discounts and billing variation, and (3) your organization's average paid rate. The gap between published and industry rates reflects structural compression the entire market faces. The gap between industry and your rate is the org-specific variance that is most actionable.

### How to Read the Charts

In each chart, the diamond marks your organization's value. The blue line marks the peer median. The purple line marks the look-alike peer median. The gray band spans P25 to P75.

### Badge Definitions

| Badge | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **On Track** | Performing at or above market -- no action needed, worth protecting |
| **At Market** | Within the typical range -- stable position |
| **Monitor** | Trend shifting or mixed signals across peer groups -- track over time |
| **Investigate** | Outside the typical range -- warrants a review with your billing or operations team |

### Key Limitation

Rate per claim measures what your organization is paid per claim submission, not per 15-minute unit of service. Organizations that bill longer sessions will show higher per-claim amounts.

## 4. Cross-Cutting Observations

### 4a. Sector Gap

Your organization operates in a sector -- community mental health centers -- that systematically collects less per claim than the broader Florida Medicaid market. On targeted case management, the CMHC median of $49.58 sits 20% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. On medication management, the CMHC median of $65.02 trails the market by 7%. Your organization's rate performance should be read in this context: matching the CMHC median is not the same as matching the market. The aspirational benchmark throughout this report is closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median, not merely matching sector peers.

### 4b. Rate Layers and Published Rates

For each benchmarked code, rate performance exists within three layers: the FL Medicaid published fee schedule rate (the ceiling), the All FL Medicaid average paid rate (what the industry actually collects), and your organization's average paid rate. For example, the published rate for behavioral therapy services (H2019) is $21.87 per 15-minute unit -- the All FL median of $71.43 reflects industry-wide compression from that per-unit rate -- and your organization's rate of $143.65 represents the org-specific variance above industry. For office visits (99214), the published rate is $53.43 per visit, the industry collects $42.75, and your organization collects $15.77. The gap between industry and published rates reflects structural compression the entire market faces; the gap between your rate and industry is the most actionable lever.

### 4c. Caseload Pattern

A recurring pattern across multiple codes is lower-than-peer patients per clinician: Targeted Case Management at 179.5 vs the CMHC median of 275.0 (34.7% below), Mental Health Assessment at 76.0 vs 115.42 (34.1% below), and Treatment Plan Development at 33.0 vs 75.0 (56.0% below). This raises the question of whether your organization's staffing model is designed around smaller, higher-touch caseloads, whether recent hiring has outpaced patient volume growth, or whether patients are being served through service lines (residential, ACT, crisis) that are not fully visible in this professional-billing data channel.

### 4d. Rate Outlier Specificity

The rate premium is code-specific, not organization-wide. Behavioral Therapy Services (H2019) at $143.65 is 101.1% above the All FL Medicaid median, while Medication Management (T1015) at $68.84 is 1.9% below the market and Targeted Case Management (T1017) at $49.69 is 20.1% below. This suggests that whatever drives the H2019 premium -- whether session structure, contract terms, or billing patterns -- does not extend to other codes, and each code warrants its own operational investigation.

### 4e. Revenue Concentration

The top three service lines -- Outpatient Therapy (40.8%), Assertive Community Treatment (26.1%), and Crisis Stabilization (23.1%) -- account for 90.0% of visible revenue. Of that, 49.2% (ACT plus Crisis) has limited or no peer benchmarking in this data channel, meaning nearly half of the visible revenue base cannot currently be evaluated against market comparisons.

## 5. Caveats and Limitations

**Peer methodology:** The look-alike methodology matches organizations by the similarity of their visible Medicaid revenue mix. It identifies peers who bill a comparable blend of outpatient therapy, ACT, and case management codes. However, it does not directly match on organizational structure. Your organization operates a crisis stabilization unit (23.1% of visible revenue) and is registered as a residential treatment facility, but most look-alike peers lack these service lines. For crisis and residential services, a service-specific peer group would provide more meaningful comparison.

**Per-claim vs per-unit:** All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes, a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units.

**Modifier rollup:** Assessment codes (H0031, H0032) combine all modifier variations into a single per-code figure.

**Residential gap:** Your organization is registered as a residential treatment facility, but no residential per-diem codes appear in this professional-billing data, so residential revenue is absent from this analysis.

**Volume limitations:** Three codes in this report have fewer than 100 claims -- H0031 (91 claims), H0032 (68 claims), and 99214 (45 claims) -- and their percentile positions should be interpreted with appropriate caution.
