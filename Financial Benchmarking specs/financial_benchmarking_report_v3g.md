### The Four Metrics

- **Rate per Claim:** What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal.
- **Patients per Clinician:** How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves. This is your caseload signal.
- **Visits per Patient:** How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment engagement signal.
- **Revenue per Patient:** Total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal.

### Peer Groups

Your organization is compared against 10 look-alike peers selected by similarity of visible Medicaid revenue mix, with similarity scores ranging from 0.7639 to 0.8559. In addition, five secondary peer groups provide broader context: All FL Medicaid Orgs, Similar CMHCs, Similar Size Orgs, Similar Market, and Same Classification. Each peer group illuminates a different dimension -- organizational type, scale, geography, or regulatory classification -- and no single group tells the complete story.

### How to Read the Charts

In each chart, the diamond marks your organization's value. The blue line marks the peer median. The purple line marks the look-alike peer median. The gray band spans P25 to P75.

### Badge Legend

| Badge | Meaning |

### Key Limitation

Rate per claim measures what your organization is paid per claim submission, not per 15-minute unit of service. Organizations that bill longer sessions will show higher per-claim amounts.

## 5. Caveats & Methodology

### Peer Methodology

The look-alike methodology matches organizations by the similarity of their visible Medicaid revenue mix. It identifies peers who bill a comparable blend of outpatient therapy, ACT, and case management codes. However, it does not directly match on organizational structure. Your organization operates a crisis stabilization unit (23.1% of visible revenue) and is registered as a residential treatment facility, but most look-alike peers lack these service lines. For crisis and residential services, a service-specific peer group would provide more meaningful comparison.

### Per-Claim vs Per-Unit

All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes, a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units.

### Modifier Rollup

Assessment codes (H0031, H0032) combine all modifier variations into a single per-code figure.

### Residential Gap

Your organization is registered as a residential treatment facility, but no residential per-diem codes appear in this data -- residential revenue is entirely absent from this analysis.

### Volume Limitations

Three codes have fewer than 100 claims in the analysis period: H0031 (91 claims), H0032 (68 claims), and 99214 (45 claims), making their metrics more sensitive to individual claim variation and less reliable as stable benchmarks.
