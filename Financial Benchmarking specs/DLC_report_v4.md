## Section 1: About This Report

### Organization Profile

Your organization is a comprehensive behavioral health system operating as both a community mental health center and a residential treatment facility, with a crisis stabilization unit and an Assertive Community Treatment team. Visible Medicaid FFS revenue is concentrated in Outpatient Therapy (40.8% / $497,851), Assertive Community Treatment (26.1% / $318,844), and Crisis Stabilization (23.1% / $281,712), with smaller shares in Case Management (6.9%), Assessment (2.4%), Psychosocial Rehabilitation (0.6%), and Medication Management (0.1%). Despite the residential facility registration, no residential per-diem codes appear in this data.

### Sector Gap Context

An important context for this report: the CMHC sector in Florida systematically collects less per claim than the broader Medicaid market on several key codes. For example, CMHC median payment for targeted case management (T1017) is $49.58 -- 20% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. For medication management (T1015), CMHCs collect $65.02 vs the market's $70.18 (7% below). This means that performing "at the CMHC median" may still represent underperformance relative to the broader market. Throughout this report, we compare against both your sector peers (similar CMHCs) and the full Florida Medicaid market. The aspirational benchmark is closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median.

### Rate Layer Context

When published fee schedule rates are available, this report frames rate performance across three layers: (1) the FL Medicaid published rate -- the maximum AHCA will pay, (2) the All FL Medicaid average paid rate -- what the industry actually collects after MCO discounts and billing variation, and (3) your organization's average paid rate. The gap between published and industry rates reflects structural compression the entire market faces. The gap between industry and your rate is the org-specific variance that is most actionable.

### Data Scope

This report analyzes $1,220,425 in Florida Medicaid fee-for-service claims billed through professional servicing clinicians during 2024. This represents one component of your organization's total revenue -- it does not include managed care organization payments, residential per-diem billing, facility-level crisis stabilization payments, commercial insurance, grants, or other funding sources. Your organization's total annual operations are significantly larger than the $1.2 million visible in this data. The benchmarks and findings below apply only to this specific billing channel.

**What is visible:** Within this $1.2 million slice, we benchmark six procedure codes where sufficient peer comparison data exists. Two additional high-volume service lines -- Assertive Community Treatment (H0040, $318,844) and Crisis Stabilization (S9485, $281,712) -- are visible in the data but cannot be benchmarked because they are billed at the facility level or have too few comparable peers. Two smaller codes (H0035, H0046) lack peer data. The six benchmarked codes total $315,054 in this billing channel.

**What is not visible:** Your organization is registered as both a community mental health center and a residential treatment facility. It operates a 36-bed crisis stabilization unit and an Assertive Community Treatment team. The majority of revenue from these programs -- residential per-diem payments, MCO contract payments, facility-level crisis billing, and non-Medicaid funding -- does not appear in this data. This report should be read as a benchmarking of your professional outpatient Medicaid billing, not as a comprehensive financial assessment.

**Claim count note:** Claim counts in this report reflect claims billed through professional servicing clinicians with a Florida practice location. Your organization's total claim volume for some codes is higher -- for example, 2,904 total H2019 claims in 2024, of which 1,254 flow through professional clinicians and are used for benchmarking.

### The Four Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Rate per Claim** | What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal. |
| **Patients per Clinician** | How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves. This is your caseload signal. |
| **Visits per Patient** | How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment engagement signal. |
| **Revenue per Patient** | Total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal. |

### Peer Groups

Each peer group is cited with its member count (n) so you can gauge the breadth of the comparison.

## Section 4: Cross-Cutting Observations

### Caseload Pattern

Patients per clinician falls below peer medians across multiple codes: T1017 at 179.5 vs the CMHC median of 275.0, H0031 at 76.0 vs 115.42, and H0032 at 33.0 vs 75.0. This pattern raises the question of whether an organizational factor -- such as staffing ratios, panel management practices, or patient flow between service lines -- is contributing to lower caseloads rather than a code-specific issue. Your team should investigate whether clinicians are carrying patients across multiple codes in ways that fragment the per-code caseload count, or whether staffing has grown faster than patient volume.

### Rate Outlier Concentration

The rate premium is concentrated in a single code: H2019 at $143.65 is nearly double the peer median, while T1015 ($68.84) and T1017 ($49.69) are at or near market rates. This raises the question of whether the H2019 premium reflects billing structure (unit bundling, session duration) rather than favorable contract terms that would apply across all codes -- if it were a contract-level factor, the premium would likely appear on other codes as well.

### Revenue Concentration and Benchmarking Gaps

Your three largest service lines -- Outpatient Therapy (40.8%), ACT (26.1%), and Crisis Stabilization (23.1%) -- account for 90.0% of visible revenue. Of that, 49.2% (ACT plus crisis) has limited or no peer benchmarking data in this analysis. This means nearly half of the visible revenue cannot be assessed against peers, and the benchmarked codes ($315,054) represent roughly 26% of visible Medicaid FFS billing.

## Section 5: Methodology and Caveats

### Peer Methodology

The look-alike methodology matches organizations by the similarity of their visible Medicaid revenue mix. It identifies peers who bill a comparable blend of outpatient therapy, ACT, and case management codes. However, it does not directly match on organizational structure. Your organization operates a crisis stabilization unit (23.1% of visible revenue) and is registered as a residential treatment facility, but most look-alike peers lack these service lines. For crisis and residential services, a service-specific peer group would provide more meaningful comparison.

### Per-Claim vs Per-Unit

All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes, a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units.

### Modifier Rollup

Assessment codes (H0031, H0032) combine all modifier variations into a single per-code figure.

### Residential Gap

Your organization is registered as a residential treatment facility, but no residential per-diem codes appear in this data -- residential revenue is not captured or benchmarked in this report.

### Volume Limitations

Three codes in this report have fewer than 100 claims: H0031 (91 claims), H0032 (68 claims), and 99214 (45 claims); rate and productivity figures for these codes are based on small samples and should be interpreted with caution.
