PROBLEM 3 Clinician Churn and Burnout

The sector picture: CMHCs carry the sector's most complex patients at the lowest effective rates — a combination that compresses clinician productivity and contributes to burnout. When rates are lower, clinicians must see more patients to generate the same revenue. When patients are higher-acuity, each visit takes more clinical time. The result: burnout that drives churn, which further constrains capacity.

Where you sit: Extreme caseloads on case management, elevated across the board

Henderson's panel sizes (beneficiaries per clinician) are well above market on every benchmarked service line. The most striking: case management at 595.6 benes per clinician — more than double the CMHC sector median.

Question: Are therapy clinicians at capacity? With above-market rates and above-market panels, each therapist generates high revenue per clinician — but is this a model that can absorb growth, or is it already at the edge?

Investigation Priorities

1. T1017 Rate Decline

Your case management rate declined from $48.16 (2019) to $44.86 (2024) while the statewide median rose. At 20.6% of visible revenue and 15,041 claims, this is your most impactful code. Investigate denial rates, modifier optimization, and contract renegotiation opportunities. Even closing the gap to the CMHC P50 ($49.58) would add ~$4.72/claim across 15,041 claims.

Impact: High. Closing to CMHC P50 = ~$71K. Closing to All FL P50 ($62.20) = ~$261K.

2. H0031 Assessment Rate at $21.40

Your assessment rate is 32.5% below the CMHC median ($31.70) and 32.3% below All FL ($31.59). The published rate for in-depth assessment is $126.11 vs $17.90 for limited. Your $21.40 average suggests a heavy limited-assessment mix. Review modifier usage and the split between HO (in-depth) and standard (limited) assessments. With 1,003 beneficiaries, even a small rate improvement has compounding effects.

Impact: Moderate. Closing to CMHC P50 = ~$10.30/claim x 1,216 claims = ~$12.5K. But the signal matters more than the dollar amount — this is your front door.

3. T1017 Caseload at 595.6 Benes/Clinician

Five case managers carry 2,978 patients — more than double the CMHC norm of 275. At declining rates ($48.16 → $44.86), this is the burnout cycle in its most concentrated form. A single departure displaces ~596 patients. Is this caseload sustainable? What is the turnover rate? Is there a clinical quality or patient outcome signal that indicates strain?

Impact: Existential for the CM line. ~$135K in revenue at risk per clinician departure.

4. H2019 Engagement Strength

Therapy RPB of $235.89 is 72% above the CMHC median of $137.38, combining an above-market rate ($79.53 vs $74.53 CMHC P50) with above-market visit frequency (2.97 vs 2.51 CPB) across 1,333 beneficiaries. This is Henderson's standout metric. Protect this strength: understand what drives the above-market rate (session duration? modifiers? contract terms?) and ensure it is sustainable under future contract cycles.

Impact: $314,445 in therapy revenue depends on maintaining this engagement depth and rate premium.

Note: 99212 at $3.76 — likely billing configuration issue

With only 16 claims and 12 beneficiaries, 99212 generates $60.12 in revenue at a rate of $3.76 — 85.8% below the All FL median of $26.52. This is almost certainly a billing configuration issue (modifier, place-of-service, or claim routing) rather than a contract problem. The dollar impact is negligible, but it may signal a broader E&M billing pattern worth reviewing.

Methodology

Data source: FL Medicaid FFS claims (DOGE/Medicaid Provider Spending), 2024. MCO encounter data not included.

Org identification: Claims matched by billing NPI to organizational entity via NPPES. Professional servicing NPIs only (entity_type_code = 1).

Classification note: Henderson Behavioral Health is classified as BH_SPECIALTY based on NPPES taxonomy codes (101YM0800X, 2084P0800X, 251B00000X, 261Q00000X), not as CMHC. For consistency with the industry landscape report, this report uses CMHC sector medians as the primary comparison where available. BH_SPECIALTY peer medians are referenced where they provide additional context.

Peer groups: CMHC sector (86 orgs), All FL Medicaid (full market), Similar BH_SPECIALTY Peers, Similar Size (Medium), Similar Market (Moderate), Look-alike peers (10 orgs, cosine similarity 0.83–0.95 on service-line revenue mix).

Metrics: Payment per claim (rate), revenue per beneficiary (engagement depth), claims per beneficiary (visit frequency), beneficiaries per clinician (panel size / productivity).

Sector context: Sector-level numbers ($8.1M rate gap, 23.6%→5.0% leakage, $157M upper-bound leakage gap) are from the FL CMHC Landscape Report v4, which benchmarks the full CMHC sector against all non-CMHC FL Medicaid providers.

Limitations: This report shows FL Medicaid FFS only — a partial view. Organization-level conclusions should be validated against internal data. Residential treatment revenue, facility-level billing (including H0040 ACT), and MCO encounter data are not captured in the per-code benchmarks. Beneficiary counts are not deduplicated across service codes.
