Executive Summary

  • This report covers $1,220,425 in Florida Medicaid fee-for-service professional claims -- a portion of your organization's total operations -- and the two largest service lines by visible revenue, Assertive Community Treatment ($318,844) and Crisis Stabilization ($281,712), cannot be benchmarked due to facility-level billing and insufficient peer data.

  • Your per-claim rate for Behavioral Therapy Services (H2019) is $143.65 -- nearly double the look-alike peer median of $73.96 (n=10) and the CMHC median of $74.53 (n=86) -- representing an $87,391 annual rate difference whose cause cannot be determined from claims data alone and warrants investigation by your billing team.

  • Office visit (99214) payments of $15.77 per claim are well outside the typical range -- 63% below the statewide median of $42.75 -- across only 45 claims, warranting a billing configuration review.

Section 1: About This Report

Organization Profile

Visible Medicaid FFS professional revenue is concentrated in Outpatient Therapy (40.8% of the $1.2M total), Assertive Community Treatment (26.1%), and Crisis Stabilization (23.1%), with Case Management (6.9%), Assessment (2.4%), Psychosocial Rehabilitation (0.6%), and E&M Office Visits (0.1%) making up the balance. Sector Gap Context

An important context for this report: the CMHC sector in Florida systematically collects less per claim than the broader Medicaid market on several key codes. For example, the CMHC median payment for targeted case management (T1017) is $49.58 -- 20% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. For medication management (T1015), CMHCs collect $65.02 vs the market's $70.18 (7% below). This means that performing "at the CMHC median" may still represent underperformance relative to the broader market. Throughout this report, we compare against both your sector peers (similar CMHCs) and the full Florida Medicaid market. The aspirational benchmark is closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median.

Rate Layer Context

When published fee schedule rates are available, this report frames rate performance across three layers: (1) the FL Medicaid published rate -- the maximum AHCA will pay, (2) the All FL Medicaid average paid rate -- what the industry actually collects after MCO discounts and billing variation, and (3) your organization's average paid rate. The gap between published and industry rates reflects structural compression the entire market faces. The gap between industry and your rate is the org-specific variance that is most actionable.

Section 4: Cross-Cutting Observations

4a. Sector Gap Pattern

The CMHC sector in Florida collects less per claim than the broader Medicaid market on several key codes. Your organization's case management rate of $49.69 is at the CMHC median of $49.58 -- but the CMHC median itself sits 20% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. For medication management, CMHCs collect $65.02 vs the market's $70.18 (7% below). For office visits (99214), however, the sector gap reverses: the CMHC median of $51.68 exceeds the All FL median of $42.75, yet your organization collects only $15.77. The question is not just where your organization stands relative to CMHC peers, but where the CMHC sector stands relative to the broader market -- and whether the sector gap is structural or addressable.

4b. Rate Layer Pattern

Your organization's rate performance varies sharply by code, and the three-layer framing (published rate, industry average, org average) helps clarify where the variance sits. For H2019, the published rate is $21.87 per 15-minute unit, the industry average is $71.43 per claim, and your rate is $143.65 -- placing your organization well above the industry norm, likely reflecting more units per claim. For T1017, the published rate is $14.82 per 15-minute unit, the industry average is $62.20, and your rate is $49.69 -- placing your organization below the industry norm and aligned with the CMHC sector's structural compression. For 99214, the published rate is $53.43 per visit, the industry average is $42.75, and your rate is $15.77 -- a gap that sits below both the industry norm and the structural compression, raising questions about billing configuration rather than contract terms.

Caseload Pattern

Patients per clinician falls below peer medians across multiple codes: T1017 at 179.5 vs the CMHC median of 275.0, H0031 at 76.0 vs the CMHC median of 115.42, and H0032 at 33.0 vs the CMHC median of 75.0. This pattern spans case management and assessment services and raises the question of whether an organizational factor -- such as staffing ratios, patient routing to residential pathways, or intake volume -- is contributing to lower caseloads across the board, rather than a code-specific issue.

Rate Outlier Specificity

The H2019 rate premium ($143.65 vs the look-alike median of $73.96) is code-specific, not organization-wide. Your T1015 rate of $68.84 is near the All FL median of $70.18, and your T1017 rate of $49.69 is at the CMHC median of $49.58. This means the premium is concentrated in a single code and raises the question of what specific billing, contract, or session-duration factor applies to H2019 but not to your other outpatient codes.

Revenue Concentration

Your top three service lines by visible revenue -- Outpatient Therapy (40.8%), Assertive Community Treatment (26.1%), and Crisis Stabilization (23.1%) -- account for 90% of the $1.2 million visible in this data. Of that, 49.2% (ACT + Crisis) has limited or no peer benchmarking available, meaning this report can comparatively assess only roughly half of your visible Medicaid FFS revenue.

Section 5: Caveats and Limitations

Peer Methodology

The look-alike methodology matches organizations by the similarity of their visible Medicaid revenue mix. It identifies peers who bill a comparable blend of outpatient therapy, ACT, and case management codes. However, it does not directly match on organizational structure. Your organization operates a crisis stabilization unit (23.1% of visible revenue) and is registered as a residential treatment facility, but most look-alike peers lack these service lines. For crisis and residential services, a service-specific peer group would provide more meaningful comparison.

Per-Claim vs Per-Unit

All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes, a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units.

Modifier Rollup

Assessment codes (H0031, H0032) combine all modifier variations into a single per-code figure.

Residential Gap

Your organization is registered as a residential treatment facility, but no residential per-diem codes appear in this data -- residential revenue is not captured in this analysis.

Volume Limitations

Three codes have fewer than 100 claims in the analysis period: H0031 (91 claims), H0032 (68 claims), and 99214 (45 claims); findings for these codes should be interpreted with appropriate volume caution.
