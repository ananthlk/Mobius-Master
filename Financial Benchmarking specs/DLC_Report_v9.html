PROBLEM 3 Clinician Churn and Burnout

The sector picture: CMHCs carry the sector's most complex patients at the lowest effective rates — a combination that compresses clinician productivity and contributes to burnout. When rates are lower, clinicians must see more patients to generate the same revenue. When patients are higher-acuity, each visit takes more clinical time. The result: burnout that drives churn, which further constrains capacity.

Determine the driver (session duration, contract terms, billing structure) to ensure it is sustainable and not at risk from contract renegotiation.

What the H2019 Rate Premium Means

Your behavioral therapy rate of $143.65 is the largest positive outlier in this report — +73.8% above even the top quartile of non-CMHC providers ($82.64). This rate has risen from $96.94 in 2019 and diverged further from peers each year. Three possible explanations (not mutually exclusive):

  1. Session duration: If your therapists bill more units per claim (longer sessions), the per-claim rate is naturally higher. Your billing team can verify by checking average units per H2019 line.
  2. Contract terms: Favorable MCO rates specific to your organization or market area.
  3. Billing structure: Different modifier usage or bundling patterns that result in higher per-claim payments.

This rate is a competitive advantage worth protecting. Combined with your therapy RPB of $360.27 (+93.7% above best-in-class), the therapy line is the strongest dimension of your organization's financial profile.

Methodology

Data source: FL Medicaid FFS claims (DOGE/Medicaid Provider Spending), 2024. MCO encounter data not included.

Org identification: Claims matched by billing NPI to organizational entity via NPPES. Professional servicing NPIs only (entity_type_code = 1).

Benchmark philosophy: This report benchmarks against a single target: Rest-of-FL P75 — best-in-class. This is the top quartile of non-CMHC FL Medicaid providers. Every rate, RPB, and panel comparison in this report measures the gap to that bar. The CMHC sector median is shown in small gray text for context only — it is never the comparison number. The rationale: the industry landscape report demonstrated that the CMHC sector systematically underperforms the broader market. Benchmarking against the sector normalizes that underperformance.

Panel benchmarks: Panel size (beneficiaries per clinician) comparisons use the best available market benchmark from the industry landscape data. These reflect non-CMHC provider norms and are labeled "Market Benchmark" throughout.

Metrics: Payment per claim (rate), revenue per beneficiary (engagement depth), claims per beneficiary (visit frequency), beneficiaries per clinician (panel size / productivity).

T1015 P75 caveat: The Rest-of-FL P75 for T1015 ($172.44) is anomalously high, likely distorted by FQHCs billing at all-inclusive encounter rates. This code is flagged with a caveat throughout the report.

Sector context: Sector-level numbers ($8.1M rate gap, 23.6%→5.0% leakage, $157M upper-bound leakage gap) are from the FL CMHC Landscape Report v4, which benchmarks the full CMHC sector against all non-CMHC FL Medicaid providers.

Limitations: This report shows FL Medicaid FFS only — a partial view. Organization-level conclusions should be validated against internal data. Residential treatment revenue, facility-level crisis billing, and MCO encounter data are not captured. Beneficiary counts are not deduplicated across service codes.
