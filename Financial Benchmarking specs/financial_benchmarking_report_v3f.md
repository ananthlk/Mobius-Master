## Executive Summary

- Your organization's H2019 (Behavioral Therapy Services) rate of $143.65 per claim is nearly double the look-alike peer median of $73.96, translating to an estimated $87,391 annual rate advantage -- the single largest finding in this report.
- Case management (T1017) faces a compound risk: the per-claim rate has declined from $59.69 to $49.69 since 2019 while peers have risen, and caseloads of 179.5 patients per clinician sit 95.5 below the CMHC peer median of 275.0, representing an estimated $44,656 in potential annual revenue if caseloads reached peer levels.
- Medication management (T1015) rates are above the CMHC peer median ($65.02) and look-alike median ($50.72) but well below the Similar Market median of $87.12 (n=265), a nuanced signal that warrants monitoring given the different billing structures in that comparison group.
- Approximately 49.2% of your organization's visible Medicaid revenue flows through ACT (26.1%) and crisis stabilization (23.1%), neither of which has sufficient peer data for professional-level benchmarking in this report.
- Mental health assessment (H0031) rates of $57.85 per claim are nearly double the look-alike peer median of $29.05 and diverging upward -- a rate strength worth protecting.

## Section 1: About This Report

### Organization Profile

Your organization also holds a residential treatment facility taxonomy, though no residential per-diem codes appear in the claims data analyzed here.

### Data Coverage

This report benchmarks six procedure codes that together account for approximately 50.8% of your organization's visible Medicaid revenue; the remaining 49.2% flows through ACT and crisis stabilization codes that lack sufficient peer data for professional-level comparison. The benchmarking uses claims attributed to professional servicing clinicians with a Florida practice location, which means some codes show lower volumes than your organization's total billing.

> Claim counts in this report reflect claims attributed to professional servicing clinicians with a Florida practice location. Your organization's total claim volume for some codes is higher (e.g., 2,904 total H2019 claims vs 1,254 used for benchmarking) because facility-level and out-of-state claims are excluded from the rate comparison.

### The Four Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Rate per Claim** | What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal. |
| **Patients per Clinician** | How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves. This is your caseload signal. |
| **Visits per Patient** | How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment engagement signal. |
| **Revenue per Patient** | Total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal. |

### Peer Groups

Your organization is compared against 10 look-alike peers selected by the similarity of their visible Medicaid revenue mix, with similarity scores ranging from 0.7639 to 0.8559. In addition to look-alike peers, five secondary peer groups provide broader context: All FL Medicaid Orgs (the full statewide pool), Similar CMHCs (organizations with community mental health center taxonomy), Similar Size Orgs (organizations with comparable claim volume), Similar Market (organizations in markets with similar provider density), and Same Classification (organizations sharing your specific classification codes).

### How to Read the Charts

In each chart, the diamond marks your organization's value. The blue line marks the peer median. The purple line marks the look-alike peer median. The gray band spans P25 to P75.

### Badge Legend

| Badge | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **Strong** | Above market median, holding steady or pulling ahead |
| **At Market** | Near the median, stable position |
| **Watch** | Above median but converging toward it, OR below median but recovering -- early warning |
| **Concern** | Below the median on most peer comparisons, not improving |

### Key Limitation

Rate per claim measures what your organization is paid per claim submission, not per 15-minute unit of service. Organizations that bill longer sessions will show higher per-claim amounts.

## Section 5: Caveats and Methodology Notes

### Peer Methodology

The look-alike methodology matches organizations by the similarity of their visible Medicaid revenue mix. It identifies peers who bill a comparable blend of outpatient therapy, ACT, and case management codes. However, it does not directly match on organizational structure. Your organization operates a crisis stabilization unit (23.1% of visible revenue) and is registered as a residential treatment facility, but most look-alike peers lack these service lines. For crisis and residential services, a service-specific peer group would provide more meaningful comparison.

### Per-Claim vs Per-Unit

All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes, a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units.

### Modifier Rollup

Assessment codes (H0031, H0032) combine all modifier variations into a single per-code figure.

### Residential Gap

Your organization holds a residential treatment facility taxonomy, but no residential per-diem codes appear in the claims data, meaning an entire category of revenue is absent from this analysis.

### Volume Limitations

Three codes have fewer than 100 claims in the analysis period -- H0031 (91 claims), H0032 (68 claims), and 99214 (45 claims) -- and their metrics should be interpreted with appropriate caution.
