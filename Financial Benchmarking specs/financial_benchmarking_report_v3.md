### What This Data Covers

The practical implication: this report is a window into your Medicaid FFS positioning, not a complete financial portrait.

### The Four Metrics

- **Rate per claim** -- What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal.
- **Patients per clinician** -- How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves in a given period. This is your caseload signal.
- **Visits per patient** -- How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment intensity or engagement signal.
- **Revenue per patient** -- The total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal, driven by both rate and visit frequency.

### Who We Compared Against

**Primary comparison: Look-alike peers.** We identified the 10 organizations in Florida whose Medicaid service-line revenue mix most closely resembles yours. These are community behavioral health systems, community mental health centers, and similar organizations with revenue spread across outpatient therapy, ACT, crisis, and case management. Similarity scores range from 0.76 to 0.86. This is the most operationally relevant peer group because these organizations face similar staffing and service delivery challenges.

**Secondary comparisons** provide additional context:

- **All Florida Medicaid providers** billing each code (288-4,211 organizations)

### How to Read the Charts

Each service code receives a badge on four dimensions:

| Badge | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Strong** | Above the market median on most peer comparisons, holding steady or pulling ahead |
| **At Market** | Near the median, stable position |
| **Watch** | Mixed signals -- above on some axes but converging, or below on some and improving |
| **Concern** | Below the median on most peer comparisons |

Special flags:
- **Anomaly** -- An unusual pattern that warrants investigation (e.g., high rate per claim but average visit frequency, suggesting differences in billing structure)
- **Volume caution** -- Fewer than 100 claims in the analysis period, meaning the sample may be too small for confident benchmarking

### Key Limitation

"Rate per claim" measures what your organization is paid each time it submits a claim, not what it is paid per 15-minute unit of service. Organizations that bill longer sessions or bundle multiple units into a single claim will show higher per-claim amounts. This is particularly important for time-based codes like individual therapy and ACT services.

## 4. Caveats and Methodology

### Data Source
This analysis is based on Florida Medicaid fee-for-service claims. It does not include managed care organization (MCO) contract rates, commercial insurance, grant-funded services, or state block grant allocations. For organizations with a significant MCO panel, the FFS picture may not be representative of overall rate positioning.

### Coverage
The claims analyzed here represent approximately 60% of the Medicaid payment landscape. Your organization is registered as a residential treatment facility, but no residential per-diem codes appear in the data. Crisis stabilization (23% of visible revenue) and assertive community treatment (26% of visible revenue) are captured but have limited peer comparison data.

### Per-Claim vs. Per-Unit
All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes (H2019, T1015, H0040, H0046), a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units. Organizations that bill longer sessions will show higher per-claim amounts. This does not necessarily mean they are receiving a higher per-unit rate. Comparing per-claim rates across organizations is meaningful for understanding revenue capture per encounter but should not be interpreted as a pure rate comparison without examining unit counts.

### Modifier Rollup
Assessment codes (H0031 and H0032) can be billed with various modifiers that indicate different assessment types or provider qualifications. This analysis combines all modifier variations into a single per-code figure. Two organizations billing the same code with different modifier patterns may show different per-claim rates for structural reasons rather than rate differences.

### Peer Group Construction
**Look-alike peers (primary):** The 10 organizations whose Medicaid service-line revenue mix is most similar to yours, based on the percentage of revenue derived from each service line. Similarity scores range from 0.76 to 0.86. These organizations include community mental health centers, community behavioral health organizations, and specialty providers. Not all look-alike peers bill every code -- ACT benchmarks include only 4 peers, and crisis stabilization has effectively no peer coverage.

**Broader peer groups (secondary):** Community mental health centers (CMHC), similarly sized organizations (medium tier), organizations in sparse markets, and all Florida Medicaid providers. These provide context but may include organizations with very different service models.

### What This Report Does Not Do
This report observes your rate and utilization positioning relative to peers. It does not diagnose the cause of any variance, and it does not make recommendations. Where the data raises a question -- such as why a rate is significantly above or below the peer median -- the report frames that question for your team to investigate using internal operational and billing data.
