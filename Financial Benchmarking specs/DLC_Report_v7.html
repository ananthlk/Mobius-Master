3. Rates: Where You Stand

Your organization's rate picture is unusual — exceptional on some codes, deeply below market on others.

Strength: H2019 at $143.65 (+92.7%)

Your behavioral therapy rate is nearly double the CMHC sector median ($74.53) and double the All FL median ($71.43). The trend data shows this rate rose from $96.94 in 2019 to $143.65 in 2024, diverging upward from peers.

Investigation: What is driving this premium — session duration (more units per claim), favorable contract terms, or billing structure? Is this rate consistent across all payers, or specific to certain MCO contracts? Your billing team can examine average units per H2019 claim to determine if this reflects longer sessions or higher per-unit rates.

Investigate: 99214 at $15.77 (-69.5%)

Your Office Visit L4 rate of $15.77 is well below both the CMHC sector median ($51.68) and the published FSI rate ($53.43). With only 62 claims and a 37-bene panel, this is a small code for your organization.

Investigation: Are these claims being billed with an incorrect modifier or place-of-service code? A rate this far below the published fee schedule suggests a systematic billing issue rather than a contract rate problem. Review the claim adjudication detail on recent 99214 submissions.

Strength: H0031 at $57.85 (+82.5%)

Your assessment rate is well above the CMHC sector median ($31.70). Both your org and the sector median sit below the published in-depth rate ($126.11/event with HO modifier) but above the limited assessment rate ($17.90). Your higher blended rate may indicate a greater proportion of in-depth (HO-modified) assessments.

What This Means

Your organization is heavily weighted toward high-acuity services (ACT + crisis = 49.2% of revenue). Compared to the sector, you have a smaller intake footprint and a larger share of intensive ongoing care. This is the opposite of the typical CMHC leakage pattern — you appear to retain (or attract) patients for ongoing high-intensity services rather than losing them after intake.

The risk is that this model depends on a small number of high-acuity programs (ACT, crisis). If those programs contract, the revenue base narrows significantly.

8. Methodology

Data source: FL Medicaid FFS claims (DOGE/Medicaid Provider Spending), 2024. MCO encounter data not included.

Org identification: Claims matched by billing NPI to organizational entity via NPPES. Professional servicing NPIs only (entity_type_code = 1).

Peer groups: CMHC sector (86 orgs), All FL Medicaid, Similar Size, Similar Market, Same Classification, Look-alike peers (cosine similarity on service-line revenue mix).

Metrics: Payment per claim (rate), revenue per beneficiary (engagement), claims per beneficiary (visit frequency), beneficiaries per clinician (panel size/productivity).

Signals: On Track at or above CMHC P50 | At Market within 10% of P50 | Monitor 10-25% below | Investigate 25%+ below or anomalous

Limitations: This report shows FL Medicaid FFS only — a partial view. Organization-level conclusions should be validated against internal data, including MCO claims, grant-funded services, and cost-to-deliver analysis. Residential treatment revenue is not captured.

This report presents benchmarking observations and investigation questions specific to your organization. It does not diagnose causes or prescribe actions. All numbers are from the canonical dataset and should be validated against internal claims data before operational decisions.
