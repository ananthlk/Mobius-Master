Section 1: About This Report

Organization Profile

Your organization is a comprehensive behavioral health specialty system based in Lauderdale Lakes, FL, operating across multiple service lines. Case Management (29.3% of visible revenue), Psychosocial Rehabilitation (26.5%), and Assertive Community Treatment (24.3%) account for 80.1% of visible Medicaid FFS revenue. Outpatient Therapy contributes 18.8%, with Assessment and Medication Management rounding out the portfolio at 1.1% and less than 0.1% respectively.

Data Scope

This report analyzes $3,277,908.71 in Florida Medicaid fee-for-service claims billed through professional servicing clinicians during 2024. This represents one component of your organization's total revenue.

What is visible: Within this $3.3 million, we benchmark codes where sufficient peer comparison data exists.

What is not visible: MCO contract payments, facility-level billing, commercial insurance, grants, and other funding sources are not included.

The Sector Gap: BH Specialty Organizations vs. the Broader Market

An important context for this report: the behavioral health specialty sector in Florida systematically collects less per claim than the broader Medicaid market on several key codes. For example, the BH specialty median payment for Targeted Case Management (T1017) is $59.78 -- still below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. For Mental Health Assessment (H0031), BH specialty peers collect $36.86 vs. the market's $31.59 (here the sector actually exceeds the market, though your organization at $21.40 falls well below both). This means that performing "at the BH specialty median" may still represent underperformance relative to the broader market on some codes. Throughout this report, we compare against both your sector peers (similar BH specialty organizations) and the full Florida Medicaid market. The aspirational benchmark is closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median.

Rate Layers: Three Levels of Comparison

When published fee schedule rates are available, this report frames rate performance across three layers: (1) the FL Medicaid published rate -- the maximum AHCA will pay, (2) the All FL Medicaid average paid rate -- what the industry actually collects after MCO discounts and billing variation, and (3) your organization's average paid rate. The gap between published and industry rates reflects structural compression the entire market faces. The gap between industry and your rate is the org-specific variance that is most actionable.

The Four Metrics

Metric What It Measures
Rate per Claim What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal.
Patients per Clinician How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves. This is your caseload signal.
Visits per Patient How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment engagement signal.
Revenue per Patient Total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal.

Section 3: Service Line Deep Dives

Targeted Case Management (T1017)

Rate per Claim -- Investigate. Your rate of $44.86 is 8.7% below the look-alike peer median of $49.12 (n=10) and 27.9% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20 (n=288). The FL Medicaid published rate for this code is $14.82 per 15-minute unit; the All FL median of $62.20 already reflects substantial industry-wide compression from that per-unit rate, meaning your organization faces both the structural compression all providers experience and an additional 27.9% org-specific gap to the industry median. Among BH Specialty peers (n=40), the median is $59.78 -- your rate sits well below the sector as well.

Patients per Clinician -- On Track. Your caseload of 595.6 patients per clinician is 130.9% above the All FL Medicaid median of 258.0 (n=288), signaling exceptionally high throughput that warrants monitoring for sustainability.

Visits per Patient -- On Track. Your utilization of 5.05 visits per patient is near the All FL Medicaid median of 5.57 (n=288).

Revenue per Patient -- Monitor. Your revenue per patient of $226.58 is 14.9% above the look-alike median of $197.18 but 37.3% below the All FL Medicaid median of $361.14 (n=288), reflecting how the rate gap compounds across visits.

Trend: Rate moved from $48.16 (2019) to $44.86 (2024). Signal: falling behind.

Operational Investigation:

  • Rate per Claim: $44.86 is 27.9% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. What is your denial rate on this code? Are you billing with the optimal modifier combination? Is this rate consistent across all your managed care contracts, or is one payer pulling the average down?
  • Patients per Clinician: 595.6 patients/clinician is 130.9% above the All FL median of 258.0. What is your clinician turnover rate for this service line? Are clinicians carrying this caseload sustainably? Is the high caseload by design or by default?

Key Question: With a rate that has declined from $48.16 to $44.86 over five years while the All FL median sits at $62.20, is the trend driven by shifts in payer mix, modifier patterns, or contract terms -- and which lever is most addressable?

Caveat: This rate reflects average payment per claim submission; a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units, so organizations billing longer sessions will appear to have higher per-claim rates.

Section 4: Cross-Cutting Observations

The Rate Gap Is Code-Specific, Not Organization-Wide

Your organization's rate performance varies dramatically by code. H2019 (Behavioral Therapy) sits 11.3% above the All FL Medicaid median while T1017 (Targeted Case Management) sits 27.9% below and H0031 (Mental Health Assessment) sits 32.3% below. This pattern indicates the rate variance is not driven by a single organizational factor (such as a blanket MCO discount) but by code-specific dynamics -- modifier usage, session length patterns, or contract terms that differ across service lines.

Revenue Concentration and Benchmarking Coverage

Your top three service lines -- Case Management (29.3%), Psychosocial Rehabilitation (26.5%), and Assertive Community Treatment (24.3%) -- account for 80.1% of visible revenue. Of that concentration, ACT (24.3%) has limited peer benchmarking data. This means roughly one quarter of your visible Medicaid FFS revenue cannot yet be compared against peers with confidence.

High Caseloads Across Multiple Service Lines

Your organization shows notably high patients-per-clinician ratios across several codes: T1017 at 595.6 (130.9% above the All FL median of 258.0), H0031 at 200.6 (141.7% above 83.0). While high throughput can reflect an efficient operating model, the pattern across multiple codes raises a question about whether staffing levels have kept pace with patient volume, and whether the high caseloads are associated with the lower-than-market per-claim rates (fewer minutes per encounter leading to lower per-claim payments on time-based codes).

Section 5: Caveats and Limitations

Peer Methodology

Peer groups are constructed using parallel single-dimension cuts: All FL Medicaid organizations, organization-billed providers, similar BH specialty peers, similar size organizations (medium), and similar market density (moderate). Look-alike peers are selected by cosine similarity on service-line revenue mix vectors (minimum similarity 0.75). Badge assignments use the BH specialty peer group as the primary reference, with All FL Medicaid as the aspirational benchmark.

Per-Claim vs. Per-Unit

All rate-per-claim figures represent the average payment per claim submission. For time-based codes, a single claim may encompass multiple 15-minute units.

Modifier Rollup

Assessment codes (H0031) combine all modifier variations into a single per-code figure.

Residential Gap

Residential per-diem codes are absent from this analysis; your organization does not carry a residential taxonomy in NPPES, and facility-level billing is outside the scope of professional claims data.

Volume Limitations

Office Visit L2 (99212) has only 16 claims and should be treated as directional only. Medication Management codes (99212, 99213) together total 55 claims and less than 0.1% of revenue.
