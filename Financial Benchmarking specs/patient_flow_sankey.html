Lost to leakage
If CMHCs retained their 23.6% intake share through ongoing care (instead of dropping to 5.0%), the sector would serve 1.37M ongoing-care beneficiaries instead of 289K.

At CMHC's own per-bene rates, that's $183M vs $38.6M in ongoing revenue alone.

The rate problem is real — $8.1M real. But closing every rate gap wouldn't change the fundamental dynamic: CMHCs assess and stabilize 24% of FL Medicaid BH patients, then 95% of the ongoing care revenue flows to other providers. For every $1 lost to rates, $19 is lost to leakage. The strategic question isn't just "how do we get paid more per visit?" — it's "how do we keep patients in care after the front door?"
