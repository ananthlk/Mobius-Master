The Four Metrics

Metric What It Measures
Rate per Claim What Medicaid pays your organization, on average, each time you submit a claim for a given service. This is your price signal.
Patients per Clinician How many unique patients each of your billing clinicians serves. This is your caseload signal.
Visits per Patient How many claims are generated per unique patient. This is your treatment engagement signal.
Revenue per Patient Total Medicaid payment received per unique patient for a given service. This is your combined economic signal.

Sector Gap Context

An important context for this report: the CMHC sector in Florida systematically collects less per claim than the broader Medicaid market on several key codes. For example, the CMHC median payment for targeted case management (T1017) is $49.58 -- 20% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. This means that performing "at the CMHC median" may still represent underperformance relative to the broader market. Throughout this report, we compare against both your sector peers (similar behavioral health specialty organizations) and the full Florida Medicaid market. The aspirational benchmark is closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median.

Rate Layers

When published fee schedule rates are available, this report frames rate performance across three layers: (1) the FL Medicaid published rate -- the maximum AHCA will pay, (2) the All FL Medicaid average paid rate -- what the industry actually collects after MCO discounts and billing variation, and (3) your organization's average paid rate. The gap between published and industry rates reflects structural compression the entire market faces. The gap between industry and your rate is the org-specific variance that is most actionable.

Peer Groups

Your organization is compared against 10 look-alike peers selected by service-line revenue mix similarity (cosine similarity range: 0.83 to 0.95). In addition, five secondary peer groups provide context: All FL Medicaid organizations, organization-billed providers, similar behavioral health specialty peers, similar-size organizations (medium), and organizations in similar market density (moderate).

How to Read the Charts

In each chart, the diamond marks your organization's value. The vertical line marks the peer group median -- the midpoint where half of peers are above and half below. The shaded band shows the middle 50% of peers -- the range within which most comparable organizations fall. If your diamond sits inside the band, you are in the typical range. If it sits outside the band, that is a signal worth investigating.

Badge Definitions

Badge Meaning
On Track Performing at or above market -- no action needed, worth protecting
At Market Within the typical range -- stable position
Monitor Trend shifting or mixed signals across peer groups -- track over time
Investigate Outside the typical range -- warrants a review with your billing or operations team

Key Limitation

Rate per claim measures what your organization is paid per claim submission, not per 15-minute unit of service. Organizations that bill longer sessions will show higher per-claim amounts.

Section 4: Cross-Cutting Observations

4a. Sector Gap

The CMHC sector in Florida systematically collects less per claim than the broader Medicaid market. For targeted case management (T1017), the CMHC median of $49.58 is 20% below the All FL Medicaid median of $62.20. Your organization's rate of $44.86 falls below even the CMHC median, placing it 27.9% below the full market. For behavioral health specialty peers specifically, the sector gap varies by code: H2019 shows a slight positive gap (+4.3%), while T1017 shows a significant negative gap (-20.3%). The aspirational benchmark throughout this report is closing the gap to the All FL Medicaid median, not merely matching sector peers.

4b. Rate Layers

Your organization's rate performance varies sharply by code, indicating the premium or deficit is code-specific, not organization-wide. On H2019, you collect $79.53 against an All FL median of $71.43 -- an 11.3% premium. On T1017, you collect $44.86 against an All FL median of $62.20 -- a 27.9% deficit. Both codes are time-based (per 15-minute unit), so the variance is likely driven by session length patterns, modifier usage, or payer mix differences rather than an organization-wide contract issue. The three-layer framing (published rate, industry paid rate, org paid rate) shows that industry-wide compression from the published fee schedule is significant on all codes, but the org-to-industry gap is where your team has the most leverage.

Revenue Concentration

The top three service lines -- case management (29.3%), psychosocial rehabilitation (26.5%), and ACT (24.3%) -- account for 80.1% of visible revenue. ACT alone (24.3%) has limited benchmarking data. This concentration means that rate or volume changes in case management or psychosocial rehabilitation have outsized impact on the overall financial picture.
