## 1. Methodology

This report outlines a structured, data-driven approach to identify and quantify revenue opportunities related to provider credentialing and roster management. The analysis adopts an "outside-in" perspective, primarily relying on publicly available data and Mobius's proprietary analytics.

**Outside-in view:** This analysis is performed from an external perspective, leveraging available public data sources including provider rosters, State Medicaid Provider Master Lists (PML), National Plan and Provider Enumeration System (NPPES) data, and historic claims data (DOGE, or Department of Government Expenditures in a general sense for Medicaid claims). We do not have access to David Lawrence Center's internal HR or credentialing system records.

**Methodology Steps:**

1.  **Organization Identification:** Identify all NPIs associated with "David Lawrence Center" from NPPES data. This includes both individual (Type 1) and organizational (Type 2) NPIs that list the organization's names.
2.  **Location Mapping:** Pinpoint all physical service locations for David Lawrence Center by cross-referencing NPPES practice addresses with known organizational addresses and service NPIs from claims data (DOGE).
3.  **Provider Association:** Associate individual providers (Type 1 NPIs) with specific organizational locations based on address matching and historical billing patterns. An "association likelihood" score is assigned to indicate the confidence of this link.
4.  **Service Type Verification:** For each location, identify the range of services (taxonomy codes) approved for Medicaid billing. This step ensures that providers are appropriately credentialed for the services they deliver at a given site.
5.  **Historic Billing Patterns Analysis:** Analyze historical Medicaid claims data to understand service volumes, total paid amounts, and average rates per claim for the organization's top-billed HCPCS codes. This provides a baseline for revenue and identifies high-impact services.
6.  **PML and Roster Validation (Mobius "Step 6"):** Conduct a series of checks against the State Medicaid Provider Master List (PML) for each NPI-taxonomy-ZIP+4 combination. This includes verifying NPI presence in PML, valid ZIP+4, approved taxonomy (against TML/PPL), and the presence of a valid Medicaid ID for the specific NPI-taxonomy-ZIP9 combination.
7.  **Taxonomy Optimization Identification (Mobius "Step 7"):** Compare each provider's currently claimed or primary taxonomy against a broader set of eligible and higher-reimbursing taxonomies for their services, identifying potential revenue uplift through credentialing optimization.
8.  **Opportunity Sizing and Prioritization:** Quantify potential revenue impacts across identified categories (A-E) by applying benchmark rates (organizational or state averages) to identified provider volumes or claim data.
9.  **Reporting and Action Plan Development:** Synthesize findings into a comprehensive report, highlighting specific opportunities, quantifying financial impacts, and recommending concrete, prioritized actions.

**Rate gap methodology note:**
Section E rate gap figures are calculated by comparing each organization's average paid rate per claim against state-wide average paid rates for the same HCPCS code. This comparison has important limitations: (1) Per diem codes (e.g., H0040 Assertive Community Treatment, S9485 Crisis Intervention) bundle multiple contacts into a single daily charge — their per-claim rates are not directly comparable to fee-for-service encounter rates that other billers may use for the same code. (2) State averages include all biller types; if an organization uses a different service model than the state average, the gap may reflect model differences rather than rate negotiation opportunities. For these reasons, per diem code gaps are shown for informational purposes and should be verified before inclusion in any revenue projection.

**Limitations:** Findings presented are indicative and based on an outside-in analysis. Data lag, enrollment timing, or roster noise can affect results. This report is intended for operational review and strategic planning, not as an audit or definitive accounting.
