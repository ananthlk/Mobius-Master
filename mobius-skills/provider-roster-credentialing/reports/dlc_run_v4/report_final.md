## 1. Methodology

This report presents a comprehensive, outside-in analysis of David Lawrence Center's provider roster and credentialing status, focusing on identifying lost or at-risk revenue opportunities. We leverage publicly available data and Mobius's proprietary analytics to generate actionable insights for both financial and operational stakeholders. We do not have access to the organization's internal HR or credentialing records.

1.  **Data Acquisition**: We begin by gathering data from multiple external sources. This includes the organization's NPIs and practice addresses from the National Plan and Provider Enumeration System (NPPES), Florida Agency for Health Care Administration (AHCA) Provider Master List (PML), Taxonomy Master List (TML), and Pending Provider List (PPL) to verify Medicaid enrollment status. Historic billing patterns and expenditure data are analyzed from publicly available claims data (DOGE claims).
2.  **Provider and Location Association**: NPIs are systematically associated with specific physical locations based on address matching and provider roster data. We categorize locations by their servicing NPI practice addresses, identifying the base organizational address and any additional servicing sites.
3.  **Medicaid NPI Initiative Compliance Checks**: For each unique NPI, taxonomy, and ZIP+4 combination ("combo"), four critical Medicaid compliance checks are performed: (1) verification that the NPI is present in the PML with an active Medicaid ID, (2) confirmation of a valid 9-digit ZIP+4 code, (3) validation that the taxonomy is approved on the TML/PPL, and (4) ensuring the NPI+taxonomy+ZIP9 combo has a valid Medicaid ID.
4.  **Opportunity Identification**: Based on the compliance checks and billing patterns, potential revenue opportunities are categorized into a "waterfall":
    *   **A. Projected Revenue at Current Run Rate**: Actively enrolled and compliant providers.
    *   **B. At-Risk Revenue**: Providers with minor compliance issues (e.g., address gaps) that jeopardize current billing.
    *   **C. Enrollment Gap**: Providers not found in the PML, indicating a lack of enrollment.
    *   **D. Taxonomy Optimization**: Providers who could potentially increase revenue by utilizing a different, higher-reimbursing taxonomy for which they are qualified.
    *   **E. Rate Gap**: Differences between the organization's average paid rates per claim and state-wide averages.
5.  **Revenue Sizing and Benchmarking**: For operational opportunities (A, B, C, D), potential revenue is calculated using an organizational billing average derived from historic claims data. This benchmark provides a standardized estimate of annual revenue potential per full-time equivalent provider (or equivalent organizational unit). For Section E, state-wide average paid rates per HCPCS code are used for comparison, where available.
6.  **Waterfall Construction**: All identified opportunities are aggregated into a revenue waterfall, providing a clear financial summary of current performance and potential gains. This structured presentation allows for prioritization based on financial impact and operational feasibility.
7.  **Limitations and Confidence Scoring**: Findings are indicative and subject to limitations inherent in outside-in data. Data lag, enrollment timing, or roster noise can affect results. This report is intended for operational review and strategic planning, not as an audit. Each finding is assigned a confidence score to guide internal verification efforts.

**Rate gap methodology note**:
Section E rate gap figures are calculated by comparing each organization's average paid rate per claim against state-wide average paid rates for the same HCPCS code. This comparison has important limitations: (1) Per diem codes (e.g., H0040 Assertive Community Treatment, S9485 Crisis Intervention) bundle multiple contacts into a single daily charge — their per-claim rates are not directly comparable to fee-for-service encounter rates that other billers may use for the same code. (2) State averages include all biller types; if an organization uses a different service model than the state average, the gap may reflect model differences rather than rate negotiation opportunities. For these reasons, per diem code gaps are shown for informational purposes and should be verified before inclusion in any revenue projection.
