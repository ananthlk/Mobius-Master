## 1. Methodology

This report utilizes an outside-in analytical approach, combining publicly available data with advanced benchmarking techniques to identify potential revenue opportunities and credentialing efficiencies for the David Lawrence Center. We do not have access to the organization's internal records for this analysis.

The methodology comprises the following steps:

1.  **Organizational Identification:** We identified the David Lawrence Center and its associated organizational NPIs using NPPES records.
2.  **Location Discovery:** Physical servicing locations were identified primarily from organizational NPI practice addresses listed in NPPES.
3.  **Provider Association:** Individual provider NPIs were associated with the identified locations based on strong address matches in NPPES. Association likelihood scores were used to filter reliable connections.
4.  **Service Mapping:** For each associated NPI at each location, we determined the likely services provided by mapping their taxonomy codes to Medicaid-approved services from the TML/PPL (Taxonomy Master List / Permitted Provider List).
5.  **Historic Billing Pattern Analysis:** We analyzed historic Medicaid claims data to understand the organization's prevalent service codes, claim volumes, and average paid rates. This forms the basis for current run rate calculations and rate gap analysis.
6.  **PML Validation & Readiness Checks:** We performed four critical Medicaid NPI initiative checks for each NPI × taxonomy × ZIP+4 combination: (1) NPI presence in the Provider Master List (PML) with a Medicaid ID, (2) valid 9-digit ZIP+4, (3) taxonomy permitted on TML/PPL, and (4) the NPI+taxonomy+ZIP9 combo having a valid Medicaid ID.
7.  **Opportunity Sizing:** Based on the readiness checks and historic billing patterns, we quantified the potential revenue impact of addressing identified gaps in enrollment, address accuracy, and taxonomy optimization.
8.  **Rate Gap Calculation:** We performed an initial analysis comparing organizational average paid rates against state-wide averages to identify potential rate negotiation opportunities.

**Rate gap methodology note:**
"Section E rate gap figures are calculated by comparing each organization's average paid rate per claim against state-wide average paid rates for the same HCPCS code. This comparison has important limitations: (1) Per diem codes (e.g., H0040 Assertive Community Treatment, S9485 Crisis Intervention) bundle multiple contacts into a single daily charge — their per-claim rates are not directly comparable to fee-for-service encounter rates that other billers may use for the same code. (2) State averages include all biller types; if an organization uses a different service model than the state average, the gap may reflect model differences rather than rate negotiation opportunities. For these reasons, per diem code gaps are shown for informational purposes and should be verified before inclusion in any revenue projection."
