## 1. Methodology

This report utilizes an outside-in analytical approach, drawing insights from publicly available datasets to evaluate the David Lawrence Center's provider roster and credentialing status. We emphasize that this analysis does not incorporate the organization's internal HR or credentialing records.

**Methodology Steps:**

1.  **Identify Organizational Presence:** We begin by identifying all associated NPIs (individual and organizational) linked to the David Lawrence Center from the NPPES database.
2.  **Map Operational Locations:** Physical service locations are identified by analyzing associated NPI practice addresses from NPPES and organizational addresses.
3.  **Associate Providers to Locations:** Individual providers are linked to the identified locations based on strong address matches, establishing a roster of active practitioners.
4.  **Determine Available Services:** For each location, we identify the range of services (taxonomies) for which the organization and its providers are approved by Medicaid, utilizing state-specific Taxonomy Master Lists (TML).
5.  **Analyze Historic Billing Patterns:** Publicly available Medicaid claims/expenditure data is analyzed to understand the volume and value of services historically billed by the organization, providing a baseline for revenue and identifying high-volume codes.
6.  **Validate Credentialing Status (FL Medicaid NPI Initiative Checks):** Each unique NPI-taxonomy-ZIP+4 combination is subjected to four critical checks against Florida Medicaid requirements:
    *   NPI presence in the Provider Master List (PML) with an active Medicaid ID.
    *   Validation of the 9-digit ZIP+4 service location.
    *   Verification that the taxonomy is permitted on the TML/PPL.
    *   Confirmation that the NPI+taxonomy+ZIP9 combination holds a valid Medicaid ID.
7.  **Quantify Opportunities:** Based on the credentialing validation results, potential revenue opportunities are categorized into a waterfall model: projected run rate (valid NPIs), at-risk revenue (address discrepancies), enrollment gaps (missing PML entries), and taxonomy optimization (underutilized taxonomies).
8.  **Benchmark Rate Gaps:** Organizational average paid rates per claim are compared against state-wide average paid rates for the same HCPCS codes to identify potential rate negotiation opportunities.

**Confidence Bands Table:**
| Band | Score | Basis | Recommended action |
| :---- | :---- | :----------------------------- | :-------------------------- |
| Perfect | 90+ | Address + billing match | Act immediately |
| Good | 70+ | Billing or strong address | Act with standard review |
| Medium | 50+ | Address match, no billing | Verify before acting |
| Low | <50 | Weak signal only | Internal verification first |

**Rate Gap Methodology Note:**
Section E rate gap figures are calculated by comparing each organization's average paid rate per claim against state-wide average paid rates for the same HCPCS code. This comparison has important limitations: (1) Per diem codes (e.g., H0040 Assertive Community Treatment, S9485 Crisis Intervention) bundle multiple contacts into a single daily charge — their per-claim rates are not directly comparable to fee-for-service encounter rates that other billers may use for the same code. (2) State averages include all biller types; if an organization uses a different service model than the state average, the gap may reflect model differences rather than rate negotiation opportunities. For these reasons, per diem code gaps are shown for informational purposes and should be verified before inclusion in any revenue projection.

**Limitations:** Findings are indicative. Data lag, enrollment timing, or roster noise can affect results. This report should be used for operational review and strategic planning, not as an audit.
