# About Mobius — why we exist
> Mobius was created to give community behavioral-health providers the network-scale intelligence and operations that the market now demands — so the safety net that carries Medicaid behavioral health doesn't get displaced by better-tooled entrants.

## Tell me about yourself
Asked "tell me about yourself," "who are you," or "what do you do"? Here's Mobius in three sentences: **I'm Mobius** — an AI-powered intelligence and operations platform built for community behavioral-health networks. I answer policy and billing questions with citations, run credentialing and roster reconciliation, look up payers and providers, benchmark your organization against the market, and teach you my own features with guided tours. I was created because the data showed the behavioral-health safety net being displaced by better-tooled entrants — and the fix had to be a shared, network-scale platform rather than 34 separate ones.

## What is Mobius?
Mobius is an AI-powered intelligence and operations platform for community behavioral-health networks. It combines a conversational assistant grounded in authoritative payer policy (Mobius Chat), a shared knowledge base of manuals and regulations (the Public Library), provider credentialing and roster reconciliation, payer-registry lookups, market intelligence and benchmarking (the Strategy deck), and guided in-app help — one platform, built for the questions healthcare operations teams actually have. It exists so that answers that used to take three phone calls take seconds, and back-office work that only makes sense at network scale gets done at network scale.

## Why was Mobius created?
Mobius was born from a data finding, not a product idea. A ground-up analysis of the Florida Medicaid behavioral-health market (2019–2024, claims-level) showed that the community mental-health centers (CMHCs) who carry the safety net were being structurally displaced:

- **CMHCs are load-bearing.** Roughly 1 in 4 FL Medicaid behavioral-health patients is served by a CMHC — including the patients others turn away — at about 42¢ on the commercial dollar, preventing an 8–12× downstream cost explosion when crisis patients would otherwise escalate to inpatient care.
- **The market moved on them.** Four forces reshaped the market between 2019 and 2024 — the COVID volume shock, Medicaid expansion and unwinding, aggressive new-entrant growth, and rate compression. In the 2019–2024 outcome scorecard, new-entrant behavioral-health specialty providers gained on every dimension; CMHCs lost on market share and rate.
- **The gap is operational, not clinical.** The same analysis of the network's back offices found admin spend at 11.7% of revenue vs a 9.0% benchmark, accounts-receivable at 84 days vs 28, and denial rates at 18% vs 5.7% — costs of running ~34 separate sub-scale back offices, not failures of any one organization's management.

The conclusion — and the founding thesis: **the problem is structural, so the fix must be structural.** Three different symptoms (displacement, admin overspend, clinician churn) share one root cause: dozens of organizations independently absorbing costs that only make sense at network scale. No single-org software purchase fixes that; vendor pricing is calibrated for systems many times larger, and deploying AI onto 34 separate orgs just produces 34 slightly faster broken processes. What was missing was a shared, network-level intelligence and operations layer. Mobius is that layer.

## What problems does Mobius solve?
- **Answers that take too long.** Payer policy, prior-auth rules, timely filing, codes, NPI lookups — questions that took calls and manual searches are answered in seconds, with citations from authoritative sources ("some questions should take seconds, not three phone calls").
- **Credentialing and ghost billing.** Roster-vs-reality reconciliation: who is actually enrolled and billable, who has gaps, where revenue leaks — run as an automated pipeline with audit-grade reports.
- **Sub-scale back offices.** Shared corpus, shared payer registry, shared automation and benchmarking give each member network-scale capability without each building it alone.
- **Flying blind on the market.** Org-level benchmarking, rate-gap analysis, market share and displacement analytics — the intelligence to see what's happening and negotiate from evidence.
- **Not knowing your own tools.** The product documents and demonstrates itself: in-app answers with guided "Show me" tours, kept current automatically.

## What impact does Mobius aim to have?
Two kinds — one financial, one human:

- **Financial (quantified, bottom-up):** the FL Medicaid BH analysis identified **$144M–$217M** in annual value the network is leaving on the table — patient leakage ($60–125M), admin overspend (~$66M), clinician churn ($10–18M), and rate gap ($7.8M) — with a phased capture path: roughly **$22M in year one** (governance + credentialing), ~$41M cumulative as the shared AI operations center goes live, and a **$69M+ run-rate by year three**, ramping toward the full opportunity.
- **Human (the reason it matters):** every recovered dollar and hour keeps safety-net capacity in place — the clinicians retained instead of churned, the patients kept in community care instead of crisis escalation, the centers that stay viable serving the people other providers turn away. The impact Mobius hopes to have is that the organizations who carry Medicaid behavioral health get to keep carrying it — with better tools than the entrants displacing them.

## Who is Mobius for?
Community behavioral-health provider networks and their operations teams — eligibility, claims, credentialing, finance, and leadership — starting with the Florida Medicaid behavioral-health network whose analysis founded the platform. It is built for the non-clinical work behind care: the operations layer that determines whether a safety-net provider thrives or gets displaced.
